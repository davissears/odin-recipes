Coffee Amaro

by James Hoffman

When the main ingredient demands respect

As a Barista turned Bartender, only to be turned back into a Barista (and in tandem an amateur Web Developer), hearing Bartenders talk about coffee has always made me wince, and vice versa. Coffee as an ingredient can be unforgiving. The window for extracting only the delicious flavors that coffee has to offer is both short and narrow, which makes mistakes easy to make.
When a someone makes a recipe that's hard to mess up, it's usually the culmination of raw experience and fleeting inspiration. Given that coffee can be so difficult to find consistency with, a recipe like James Hoffman's Coffee Amaro stands out. A bit of interest in sprits, a lot of coffee knowledge, and willingness to fuck around & find out has yielding a recipe that will live in my rolodex indefinitely. Learn more about James, and his madness HERE .

    Ingredients

  • 200g Bourbon (Good, but not great. Buffalo Trace, Makers Mark, Bulleit etc)
  • 40g coffee (James prefers something roasted for espresso here)
  • 55g whole milk
  • 1.5g bitter orange peel (dried)
  • 1.5g Gentian root
  • 0.5g fresh grated Vietnamese Cassia bark
  • 0.5g fresh grated nutmeg
  • 1 allspice berry (crushed)
  • 0.5g minced Madagascan vanilla pod
  • 60g Demerara sugar syrup (made from 2 parts sugar to 1 part water)
  • Saline solution (20g salt to 80g water)
  • Orange peel (optional to garnish)

    Steps

  1. Gather equipment
    • Cream whipper (ISI branded ones strongly recommended)
    • 3 x cream whipper charges
    • A fine mesh sieve
    • Pour over brewer and papers
    • Spoons
  2. Grind the coffee close to espresso fine.
  3. Add the bourbon and the ground coffee to the cream whipper
  4. Swirl to combine
  5. Charge the whipper with one charge.
  6. Wait 4-5 minutes
  7. Rapidly release the pressure and strain with the sieve
  8. In a separate container, first pour in the milk and then pour in the coffee infused bourbon while stirring constantly
  9. Leave for 5 minutes, or up to 2 hours in the fridge
  10. Strain the mixture through a sieve into a filter paper to clarify
  11. Return the clarified liquid to the clean cream whipper
  12. Add spices, botanicals and vanilla pod
  13. Charge, release, then charge again. Wait 5 minutes, up to 30 minutes.
  14. elease pressure and strain through a paper filter
  15. Add the sugar syrup
  16. Bottle

    Serving Suggestion

  • Pour over ice, and garnish with orange oil expression from orange peel
  • 1 part coffee amaro, to three parts good tonic water, serve on ice.
  • Add a 30ml to a Pina Colada (a personal favorite)
